Olga Teresa Tañón’s wealth amounts to $10 million


Biography of Olga Teresa Tañón

Born on April 13, 1967, in Santurce, Puerto Rico, Olga Teresa Tañón Ortíz is a well-known singer. She is famous for being a member of the highly successful Latin American group, Chantelle, and for her solo career with hits like “Aunque Tú No Quieras” and “Basta Ya.” Her musical journey started in 1987.

Have you ever pondered about Olga Tañón’s financial status as of early 2017? According to reliable sources, her net worth is estimated to be over $10 million. This wealth stems from her thriving career in the music industry, both as part of successful groups and as a solo artist. Additionally, her earnings come from minor roles in films, as well as providing soundtracks for movies and video games.

Olga Tañón’s Net Worth – $10 Million

Olga grew up in a middle-class household as the youngest of four siblings, raised by her parents, José Tañón and Carmen Gloria Ortíz. She attended school in the Levittown district of Toa Baja in Puerto Rico.

Her passion for music started at a young age. Despite not understanding the English language, she was captivated by American pop songs. During high school, Olga auditioned for the merengue band, Las Nenas de Ringo y Jossie, showcasing her talent and kickstarting her journey to increase her net worth.

After leaving the group, she joined the renowned Latin American band, Chantelle, and had a major hit, “Aunque Tú No Quieras,” which significantly boosted her net worth.

Olga caught the attention of the record label WEA Latina during that time, leading to a solo career. Her debut studio album, “Sola,” featuring songs like “Presencié Tu Amor,” “Quiero Estar Contigo,” and “Me Cambio Por Ella,” added to her wealth.

Over the years, Olga released an album annually, garnering platinum records and blending various styles like salsa and merengue, further increasing her net worth. The album “Nuevos Senderos” propelled her to international fame, making her the first Puerto Rican artist to sell over 500,000 records. The hit song “Basta Ya!” from that album topped the charts.

In 2000, Olga recorded the concert “Olga Viva…Viva Olga,” receiving recognition from the National Foundation of Popular Culture. She went on to release more albums, tour numerous countries in the USA and Europe, and recently launched the album “Una Mujer” in 2013. Her latest album, “Olga Tañón… Y Punto!” is anticipated for release in 2017, contributing to the upward trend of her net worth.

Olga Tañón’s musical accomplishments have earned her numerous awards, making her one of the most decorated Puerto Rican singers. She boasts accolades such as two Grammy Awards, three Latin Grammy Awards, and 29 Premio Lo Nuestro Awards.

In her personal life, Olga Tañón has been married to Billy Denizard since 2002, and they share two children. She was previously married to Juan Gonzalez from 1998 to 2000, with whom she has one child. Beyond her music career, Olga is recognized for her humanitarian and social activism.
